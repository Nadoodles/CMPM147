Description

What are you trying to acomplish with this experiment?

We are trying to recreate the screen of a tv when it has no signal. From top to bottom, the tile moves in random colors, giving the static feel. Of course it's also endless, so you can move around the world.


Technical

Describe in detail what you did technically and how it works?

The p3_worldKeyChanged function initializes the worldSeed variable by hashing the provided key using XXH. This ensures deterministic terrain generation by setting the noise and random seeds. The tile dimensions, defined by p3_tileWidth and p3_tileHeight, establish each tile as a 5x5 square. p3_drawTile renders tiles with colors determined by their position and the world seed, employing a hash-based selection process that creates a pseudo-random array of colors. Additionally, a TV static effect is simulated in p3_drawTile by applying dynamic translations to each tile's position, producing visual distortion reminiscent of static interference.

Statement

For this world, we wanted to create an imitation of TV static. We realized the potential of creating motion through the use of sinusoidal, cosinusoidal, and tangential functions. After experimenting with the tangent function, we found that translating this function resulted in a glitching effect that was perfect for creating our vision of TV static. The six colors are supposed to represent the pixels on a TV and the black border is an imitation of the actual TV screen.
