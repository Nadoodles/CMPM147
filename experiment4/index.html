Description

What are you trying to acomplish with this experiment?

For the first experiment, I wanted to create an infitie world that's similar to that from Minecraft. So your character is the Minecraft Steve and your job is to explore the endless world. There are currently three types of biomes. hills(Brown), Forest (dark green), and valley (light green). There's also bodies of water and lava.


Technical

Describe in detail what you did technically and how it works?

To create these bomes, I used perlin noise. So depending on each probability, will determine which part of the world is painted by a specific biome. I also added some tecture so it wasn't just plain tiles.


Reflection

Im definitely proud of this experiment. I went from just having green and brown tiles, to creating something that was genuinely random. There's also a character, and as you are moving across the world, some of the tiles move as well.


Statement

When I was brainstorming ideas, the first thing that came to mind was Minecraft. Minecraft is notorious for its infinite world where everything is never the same. With multiple biomes, and endless adventure, I tried to recreate that with this experiment. I added a character, similar to Steve, the character, and gave him the option to roam the infinite world. As you are moving, the tiles do move, especially with the forest tiles. Explore the magical world of bootleg Minecraft.
